## What Is Formal Attire for a Wedding?

"Formal wedding attire sits between cocktail and black tie on the wedding dress code scale. It's slightly more elevated than cocktail attire and a touch less dressy than black-tie nuptials," says Alex Rivera, a stylist at [Stitch Fix](https://go.skimresources.com/?id=2866X1446373&isjs=1&jv=15.9.1&sref=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.theknot.com%2Fcontent%2Fformal-wedding-attire&url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.stitchfix.com%2F&xs=1&xtz=360&xuuid=b8e169bfd57eeecf1a5605cf8e84eb70&xjsf=other_click__contextmenu%20%5B2%5D), a personal styling service. Formal [wedding attire](https://www.theknot.com/content/what-to-wear-to-a-wedding) (similar to [black-tie optional attire](https://www.theknot.com/content/what-to-wear-formal-black-tie-optional-black-tie-invited)) offers a bit more flexibility than black tie, so ankle-length dresses and dressy separates are acceptable, as well as fitted, darker-hued suits paired with a crisp white button-down and tie. "If nothing else, make sure your dress is full-length," says Caitlin Elabassi, designer for [Petal & Pup](https://click.linksynergy.com/deeplink?id=9ibcrDa*U1o&mid=45494&u1=TK-formal-wedding-attire&murl=https%3A%2F%2Fpetalandpup.com%2F). "This is one of the most important elements when it comes to a formal dress code."

While shopping for a formal attire wedding, start by considering the venue and season. "These two important details will help provide an idea for the ambiance, the couple's personal taste, and ensure you've dressed appropriately for the occasion," explains Rivera. For summer events, lighter fabrics and brighter colors are common for formal attire. On the flip side—if you're attending a winter wedding—darker shades, thicker fabrics and longer sleeves are the way to go. Prioritize seasonally appropriate pieces that are elevated and fancy, but still feel like you.

Unlike [black-tie attire](https://www.theknot.com/content/what-to-wear-black-tie), you have much more flexibility when it comes to colors and embellishments. "Don't be afraid to have a little fun with your fashion either through color or a playful print," says bridal stylist [Gabrielle Hurwitz](https://gabriellehurwitz.com/#/). "I also love the idea of a jumpsuit, suit or a patterned suit or a tuxedo with a standard necktie rather than a bow tie." As for accessories, Hurwitz says details like colorful shoes, patterned ties, statement jewelry or fun sunglasses (for outdoor weddings) are all ways to add personality to your ensemble.

Overall, pick an outfit that you truly love and feel your best in, while still being respectful of the couple's attire request. As for [colors to avoid for formal weddings](https://www.theknot.com/content/wearing-white-to-wedding), steer clear of "white, ivory, cream, or champagne-colored dresses and light-colored suits," says Rivera. Elabassi echoes this formal wedding attire rule: "As long as it's not white, most colors are safe. Guests can have much more fun with color, and many people are no longer following the traditional 'rules' of the past. If you're ever unsure, it's always best to check with the couple." You should also [avoid pieces](https://www.theknot.com/content/what-not-to-wear-to-a-wedding) that may read as too casual, such as a "cotton sundress or sports coat with chino pants," advises Hurwitz.
